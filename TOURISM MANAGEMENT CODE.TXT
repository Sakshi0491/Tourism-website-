<h1>TAJ MAHAL</h1>

<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 195349.png" alt="Centered Image" height="400" width="400">
<p>The <b>Taj Mahal</b> is a magnificent white marble mausoleum located in <b>Agra,</b>, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor <b>Shah Jahan</b> in memory of his beloved wife, <b>Mumtaz Mahal</b>, between 1632 and 1653. This architectural masterpiece is celebrated as one of the <b>Seven Wonders of the World</b> and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is famous for its perfect symmetry, grand central dome, and four tall minarets standing on the banks of the <b>Yamuna River</b>.</p>
<p>The structure is a brilliant blend of <b>Islamic, Persian, and Indian</b> architectural styles. Its walls are decorated with beautiful calligraphy and <b>Pietra Dura</b> (intricate stone inlay work) using semi-precious gems. Surrounded by a large Mughal garden called the <b>Charbagh</b>, the Taj Mahal remains a global symbol of eternal love and attracts millions of visitors every year.</p>

<h1>GOA</h1>
<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 193233.png" height="400" width="400">
<p>Goa is India's smallest state, located on the west coast. It is world-famous for its beautiful <b>beaches</b>, like Calangute and Palolem, and its relaxed lifestyle. Panaji is the capital, and the main language spoken is <b>Konkani.</b></p>
<p>The state has a unique culture because it was ruled by the <b>Portuguese</b> for 450 years. This history is visible in its colorful houses and grand churches, such as the Basilica of Bom Jesus. Besides tourism, Goa is known for <b>Dudhsagar Falls</b>, spice plantations, and delicious seafood like fish curry.</p>

<h1>JAIPUR PALACE</h1>
<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 201223.png" height="400" width="400">
<p>The City Palace in Jaipur is a grand royal complex built by <b>Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II</b> in 1729. It is famous for its beautiful blend of <b>Rajput and Mughal architecture</b>, featuring colorful courtyards and grand gates like the famous Peacock Gate.</p>
<p>A portion of the palace is still home to the royal family, while the rest is a <b>museum</b> displaying royal costumes and massive silver urns. Located in the heart of the "Pink City," it remains one of Rajasthan's most iconic historical landmarks.</p>

<h2>Hotels Near Taj Mahal</h2>

<h3>Taj Hotel Agra</h3>
<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 215651.png" height="400" width="400">

<p>Comfortable stay near Taj Mahal with great facilities.</p>
<p>The Taj Hotel in Agra, officially called Taj Hotel & Convention Centre, is a luxury 5-star property located very close to the iconic Taj Mahal, making it one of the most preferred stays for tourists visiting the city. Situated on Taj East Gate Road, the hotel offers modern, elegant rooms and suites with contemporary interiors, large windows, and premium amenities designed for comfort and luxury. Many rooms and public areas provide stunning views of the Taj Mahal, especially from its famous rooftop infinity pool and sky lounge.<p>



<h2>Hotels Near Jaipur Palace</h2>

<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 215916.png" height="400" width="400">

<p>Luxury hotel with royal Rajasthani experience.</p>


<p>In Jaipur, often called the Pink City, “royal hotels” usually refer to heritage properties inspired by Rajasthan’s palace culture rather than a single hotel name. One popular option is Hotel Royal Orchid Jaipur, a modern 5-star hotel near the airport that combines luxury rooms, rooftop pool, and fine dining, making it ideal for both tourists and business travelers. For a more traditional royal experience, Royal Heritage Haveli Jaipur offers a boutique stay in a restored haveli with classic architecture, courtyards, and a peaceful atmosphere reflecting Rajasthan’s royal past. <p>

<h2>Goa</h2>
<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 220931.png" height="300" width="300">
<h2>FISH CURRY DISH</h2>
<p>
Goa is famous for seafood dishes like <b>Fish Curry Rice</b> and Prawn Balchão.
These foods have a unique blend of Indian and Portuguese flavors.
</p>
<p>Fish curry rice is a classic coastal Indian dish made of spicy, tangy fish curry served with steamed rice, and it’s especially popular in regions like Goa, Kerala, Maharashtra, and West Bengal. The dish varies by region, but it generally combines fresh fish cooked in a flavorful gravy made with spices, and is eaten with plain rice to balance the richness<p>.


<h2>Jaipur</h2>
<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-03 220807.png" height="300" width="300">
<img src="C:\Users\GOVINDA COMP YEOLA\Pictures\Screenshots\Screenshot 2026-04-06 084227.png" height="300" width="300">
<h2>GHEVAR</h2>
<p>
Jaipur is known for traditional dishes like Dal Baati Churma and Ghewar.
These foods are rich in taste and represent royal culture.
Ghevar is a famous traditional sweet from Rajasthan, especially popular in cities like Jaipur. It is a disc-shaped dessert made from flour, ghee, and sugar syrup, known for its unique honeycomb-like texture that is both crisp and juicy.<p>

<h2>PETHA</h2>
<p>
Agra is famous for sweets like Petha and Mughlai dishes like Biryani.
These foods reflect Mughal culture and are loved by tourists.
Petha is a famous North Indian sweet that originates from Agra and is closely associated with the city’s culinary identity, much like the Taj Mahal is with its history. It is a translucent, soft candy made from ash gourd (also called winter melon), sugar, and flavorings.
